Title: World War I (1914-1918)
Also Known As: The Great War
Belligerents: 
- Allied Powers: France, Great Britain, Russia, Italy, Japan, and the United States (joined 1917).
- Central Powers: Germany, Austria-Hungary, Ottoman Empire, and Bulgaria.

Primary Causes:
1. Militarism: An arms race in Europe, particularly between Germany and Britain.
2. Alliances: Complex treaties (Triple Entente vs. Triple Alliance).
3. Imperialism: Competition for colonial territories in Africa and Asia.
4. Nationalism: Intense pride and desire for self-determination in the Balkans.
Immediate Trigger: The assassination of Archduke Franz Ferdinand of Austria-Hungary in Sarajevo 

Key Warfare Characteristics:
- Trench Warfare: Static, defensive lines primarily on the Western Front.
- Attrition: Strategy of wearing down the enemy's resources and manpower.
- New Technology: First major use of tanks, airplanes, chemical weapons (poison gas), and submarines (U-boats).

Major Battles:
- Battle of the Marne (1914): Halted the German advance into France.
- Battle of Verdun (1916): One of the longest and costliest battles in history.
- Battle of the Somme (1916): Noted for massive British casualties on the first day.

End of War:
- Armistice signed on November 11, 1918.
- Treaty of Versailles (1919): Imposed heavy reparations and "War Guilt" on Germany.
- Outcome: Collapse of the Russian, German, Austro-Hungarian, and Ottoman Empires.
