Title: The Cold War (1947-1991)
Definition: A state of political and military tension between the Western Bloc (USA and NATO) and the Eastern Bloc (USSR and Warsaw Pact). It was "cold" because there was no large-scale direct fighting between the two superpowers.

Key Ideologies:
- Western: Capitalism and Democracy.
- Eastern: Communism and Totalitarianism.

Core Doctrines:
- Containment: US policy to prevent the spread of Communism.
- Truman Doctrine (1947): Support for nations resisting Communism.
- Marshall Plan: Economic aid to rebuild Western Europe.
- Mutually Assured Destruction (MAD): The nuclear stalemate where both sides feared total annihilation.

Major Events:
- Berlin Blockade & Airlift (1948): First major crisis of the Cold War.
- Korean War (1950-1953): The first major proxy war.
- Cuban Missile Crisis (1962): The closest the world came to nuclear war.
- Vietnam War (1955-1975): Prolonged conflict involving US intervention.
- Space Race: Competition for spaceflight supremacy (Sputnik to Moon landing).

End of the Cold War:
- Fall of the Berlin Wall (1989): Symbolic end of the division of Europe.
- Reforms: Mikhail Gorbachev's Glasnost (openness) and Perestroika (restructuring).
- Dissolution: The Soviet Union collapsed in December 1991.
