Title: World War II (1939-1945)
Belligerents:
- Allied Powers: Soviet Union, United States, United Kingdom, China, and France.
- Axis Powers: Nazi Germany, Imperial Japan, and Fascist Italy.

Primary Causes:
1. Failure of the League of Nations.
2. Harsh terms of the Treaty of Versailles.
3. The Great Depression leading to the rise of totalitarian regimes.
4. Policy of Appeasement toward Hitler's expansion.
Immediate Trigger: German invasion of Poland on September 1, 1939.

Key Turning Points:
- Battle of Britain (1940): Air battle that prevented German invasion of the UK.
- Operation Barbarossa (1941): German invasion of the Soviet Union.
- Pearl Harbor (Dec 7, 1941): Japanese attack bringing the USA into the war.
- Battle of Stalingrad (1942-43): The decisive turning point on the Eastern Front.
- D-Day / Operation Overlord (June 6, 1944): Allied invasion of Normandy.

Human Rights & Atrocities:
- The Holocaust: Systematic, state-sponsored genocide of six million Jews and others by the Nazi regime.

End of War:
- V-E Day (May 8, 1945): German surrender.
- Atomic Bombs: Dropped on Hiroshima and Nagasaki in August 1945.
- V-J Day (August 15, 1945): Japanese surrender.
- Outcome: Emergence of the USA and USSR as rival superpowers; creation of the United Nations.
